09 / Limits & next steps
Industry × NoiPA
Trade-offs and limits

What we chose, what we left out,
what comes next.

01
Streamlit → React + FastAPI.
The original demo was a Streamlit app. We replaced it because Streamlit could not deliver a node-by-node live pipeline view nor a clean before/after score animation. Cost: a separate frontend codebase. Worth it.
02
Groq → Ollama → DeepSeek.
Three providers tested, one chosen. Groq rate-limits broke our four-call multi-agent pattern. Ollama on Colab disconnected mid-run. DeepSeek-Chat (V4) hit the right quality / cost / availability balance. Branches feature/agents-data-quality, ollamacolab, deepseek in the repo carry the traces.
03
check_cross_column is NoiPA-specific.
The only tool whose rules are hardcoded against the four assigned fixtures. The synthetic generalisation test surfaces this honestly. Next step: promote it to a discovered ruleset, à la discover_dataset_rules.
10 / Take-away
Industry × NoiPA
Take-away

Our main points.

01 / scale
4 LLM agents + 8 deterministic tools = 12 nodes, ~3 500 tokens per dataset.
02 / lift
Reliability +43 average across 5 datasets, including one the system never saw.
03 / trust
Every agent has a deterministic fallback. The pipeline runs even when the LLM provider does not.
